What is the Definition of Wealth?

First, let's look at the components that make up wealth.

  1. Healthy Cash Flow
  2. Healthy Lifestyle
  3. Entrepernurial Mindset

There are many definitions for wealth, but let's really look at what it means to be wealthy. Base on my observation and understanding, wealth is not measured in cash; because, by itself is a bi-product and not a component. Cash only defines riches, so does possessing things.

So what is my definition? You might ask. Wealth or true wealth, in my eyes; is a balance between having a healthy cash flow, living a healthy lifestyle and having a entrepernurial mindset. Having a healthy cash flow means to have a greater flow of cash coming in than going out. It also means that you are no longer a slave to money, and you are making wiser financial decisions.

When I say lifestyle, I'm talking about; living life the way you want to live it, do whatever you want to do, whenever you want do it, with whomever you want to do it with. Without this component; one will just be rich, having loads of money and assests and still feel unacomplished, miserable and lonely. Why? Because, there will be no one to share your success with, people need other people and that's just it.

The third component is like the engine to a car. A car is just a shell with shiny rims and looking beautiful on the outside, but can't go nowhere without a engine. It is the same with having wealth without a entrepernurial mindset. This key component is what seperates the men from the boys, it works without been seen, while the outside is shiny and beautiful and everyone can see the results; the engine is doing all the dirty work, same it is for the entrepernurial mindset.

Why is the Wealthy in the 1% and the Poor and the Middle Class in the 99%?

First, let's define what I mean by 1% and 99%. In a section in his book, Cash Flow Quadrant, Robert Kiyosaki wrote about where do you live. He illustrates that through a quadrant, which was labeled; 'E'.'B'.'S'.'I', with 'E' meaning employed, 'B' meaning business owner, 'S' meaning self employed and 'I' meaning investor. All representing a address, do you know where you live?

However; he was saying that on the left side, resides the 'E' and the 'S' population which is 99% of the world's population and they earn 2% of the world's dollar. Over on the right side, resides the 'B' and the "I" population which is 1% of world's population and they earn 98% of the world's dollar.

Now that we are on the same page, let's figure out together why this is so.

Robert Kiyosaki said that; "The main reason why people struggle finacially is because they have spent years in school, but learn nothing about money. The result is that people learn to work for money.....but never have money worked for them." Look at the diagram below:

There are two kinds of people within the business world:

  1. Prosumer
  2. Consumer

A Prosumer is typically a producer and a consumer, this person develops and maintain a healthy cash flow by keeping his or her incoming cash flow greater than his or her outgoing cashflow. Meaning that they invest more and splurge less, they also have the entrepernurial mindset to keep producing.

A consumer on the other hand, does the opposite. Their outgoing cash flow out weighs their incoming cash flow.

Now you see that a 1 percenter will always be a 1 percenter, but the good news is; a 99 percenter can also become a 1 percenter. All it takes is a change of mindset and perspective, then everything else wil fall into place. Let's change that address from 'E' and 'S' to 'B' and 'I'.
