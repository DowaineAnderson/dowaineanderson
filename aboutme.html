Let me tell you a story.

Long long time ago, around thirty plus years, from humble beginings, in a small community resting deep in the hills of Saint Catherine; a bouncing baby boy was given birth to in Kingston and was raised in Saint Catherine. As a child growing up, he was very hardworking. This began at home, as he would willing take part in chores, tend to the farm, among any other tasks that were given to him. He diligently did all his duties without complaining, thus gaining the appreciation for work in general and it also thought him core values which helped him throughout his life. Having gained that experience and appreciation from home; it translated throughout his childhood, especially his church and school life.

At school; he participated in tending to the school farm where they had their own live stock (cattle and chicken). At church, it was no different; he participated in numerous activites and one of those activities was playing music (today he is a skilled drummer). Growing up without a father wasn't a big deal, until he went to high school. This realization, took it's toll right through out the latter part of high school. This lad was however a good behaving child and very quiet, despite not having a father figure in his life. As he went on with his life and upon leaving high school, he was not at the legal age to get a job. So he repeated grade ten at another high school.

At the second high school, this young lad joined the track and field team, to get a free pass to attend the school inorder to relieve his mother from paying the school fee. When the final year came, he got his first job as a pump attendant at Bourne's Texaco Service Station. With a mindset of wanting more out of life, he never stayed there long. He then went to further his education, at the Caribbean Maritime Institute (currently known as Caribbean Maritime University) to make himself more marketable for the working world. Growing up for this lad wasn't easy, niether was adulthood; his second job pushed him to be more responsible as he starts to see the bigger picture and perspectives changed. As this lad continues to grow personally and mentally, it spur something within him; he started to become more business minded, and as a result, his mindset, values and interests starts to gravitate towards that angle.

This gravitational pull, caused him to start venture down the entrepernurial road. He is now the founder and CEO of Safe Journey Car Rental Services, chief musician at his church, just to mention a few achievements attained; after having gone through faliure, after failure, after failure. He is also looking to open other doors to achieving his dreams which is to build a legacy for generations to come, because he never stop trying and now you are reading just a small part of his story, that lad name is Dowaine Anderson.

About Us

In life, there are two categories of people;

  1. The 1% category (the wealthy)
  2. The 99% (the poor and middle class)

These will be broken down for you later. It was mentioned in the book, Cash Flow Quadrant, written by Robert Kiyosaki that these two main categories, attracts a certain mindset. But as time changes, more and more persons in the 99% category are making or looking to make that mindset shift. Therefore, they are seeking creative ways to build their wealth in order to become apart of the 1% category.

Wealth Toll Booth (WTB) is a mentorship club, created to mentor and educate those persons on how to build long-lasting personal and generational wealth by switching and adopting the 1% mindset, and strategically place 'toll booth(s)' on the 'rodways' of their life, where 'financial traffic' normally flows. Hence; WTB mentorship club will help bridge the gap, by building a community where applied knowledge about wealth creation can be shared and in the long run, applied by those needing to advance themselves; but in order to do that, WTB will break down certain phrases that was used, as to give you a clear brief understanding and out look.

A 'toll booth' in this context; is symbolic to the the areas in everyday life that manages and directs 'financial traffic', such as; investments, afiliate marketing, business ownership, e.t.c. A toll booth in the real world, manages and directs the movement of vehicular traffic. Let's now look at 'roadway'; a 'roadway' in this context, is symbolic to the areas in life that is intended for 'fincial traffic' such as; income generating avenues. A roadway in general is only that portion of a road, intended for normal vehicular traffic. 'Financial traffic', as it is coined in this context, is symbolic to the areas in life that measures the inward and outward cash flow. Whenever the inward cash flow outweighs the outward cash flow, there is a build up of finances, causing 'financial traffic' and these measures would include having more than one income streams.
